> I visited each competitor's actual site with a real browser and read their live feature and

## TL;DR — three things that should change your plan

1. **Your ₹499 flat plan is not a price advantage. It's mid-pack with the fewest features.**
   Replix (Telugu, direct competitor) is **₹399**. Clipdify is **$6.99 (~₹600)** with vastly more.
   ButterCut Creator is **₹599**. You are priced *at* the market while delivering *less* surface.

2. **A direct Telugu competitor already owns your exact caption feature — and has the distribution
   you don't.** Replix Captions does word-by-word Telugu + Tanglish burn-in, is founder-led by an
   actual Telugu creator (90k YT / 180k IG), and shows named testimonials from creators at
   **401K, 287K, 229K followers**. That's the GTM you're missing.

3. **But Replix does NOT do long-form → clips.** It captions a Reel/Short you already have
   ("Drop a Reel or Short"). **Your clipping pipeline is the real differentiation** — not captions.
   Reposition around that.

## What I'd change, given this

1. **Reposition.** Not "Telugu captions" (Replix owns that narrative). Own
   **"drop a 2-hour Telugu podcast, get 10 publish-ready Shorts."** That's the thing none of the
   Telugu-native tools do.
2. **Fix pricing to match the market shape — everyone meters, nobody sells flat-unlimited.**
   Free (watermarked, minutes-capped, short expiry) → Creator ~₹399–499 (metered minutes,
   watermark-free) → Studio ~₹999. Add a **₹99 one-time pack** — ButterCut and Replix both run one.
3. **Steal Replix's conversion mechanic:** let free users *preview* every premium style in the
   editor and gate only the **export**. Cheap to build, converts far better than a feature table.
4. **Reframe your 24h TTL as a free-tier limit, not a silent bug.** Opus expires free clips in
   3 days, Replix in 1 hour, ButterCut in 7–14 days. Expiry is *normal* — silently deleting a
   **paying** user's library is not. Make it a published tier boundary.
5. **Ship the table-stakes four:** filler/silence removal, surfaced virality score, auto
   emoji/keyword highlight, real social publishing (your share buttons are dead; Clipdify posts to
   six platforms).
6. **Run the proof no one has run.** Nobody — not the vendors, not any reviewer — has benchmarked
   actual Telugu conjunct rendering across these tools. Put the same clip through Shotvi, Replix,
   Clipdify and ButterCut and screenshot the వత్తులు. If yours is correct and theirs break, that
   comparison **is** your marketing, your moat proof, and your fundraising slide. If theirs are
   fine, you need to know that immediately — because then the caption moat isn't real and clipping
   is all you have.

## The four lessons worth stealing

### 1. 🥇 Opus puts the product's first action *in the hero* — pre-login
Their hero is: **"1 long video, 10 viral clips. Create 10x faster."** and directly beneath it a live
input: **`Drop a video link` → [Get free clips] or [Upload files]`**. No signup wall. You paste, it
works, *then* you're hooked.

**You have this exact mechanic — but only *after* login** (`FirstRunHero`). Your public landing page
instead offers "Watch 60-sec demo," **which does nothing** (dead `onClick`, flagged in the review).

> **Highest-leverage UI change available to you:** move the URL-paste box into the public landing
> hero and let a first clip render watermarked before signup. You already have every piece.

### 2. Clipdify ships a *real* timeline editor
Their editor screenshot shows a genuine filmstrip timeline: frame-numbered ruler, video thumbnails
across the track, zoom slider, snap/magnet toggles, per-track mute/visibility/camera controls,
"Main scene" label. It reads like CapCut.

**Yours renders 120 bars from `Math.sin(i*0.35)`** — a decorative fake waveform (P2 in the review).
Against this, your timeline looks like a placeholder. Real waveform + word ticks would close it.

### 3. ButterCut surfaces metering *inside the product*
Their app screenshot shows a persistent **`Credits 1320`** chip in the top bar. Usage is always
visible, so upgrading feels like a natural consequence rather than a paywall ambush. They also push
a **QR code → mobile app** overlay.

**You have no metering at all**, and no mobile anything. When you add usage limits, put the counter
in the top bar from day one.

### 4. Replix's trust-building is what yours is faking
Theirs: a **real founder** ("Built by @ssktechy", with his actual channel sizes), real creator handles
with follower counts (**@techwithyesh 401K, @rishikesav_m 287K, @manjuaitalks 229K**), and Telugu-language
testimonials left in Telugu. Hero shows **a real creator's face** with a live word-by-word Tanglish
caption running over it.

**Yours:** "12,000+ creators," "94 Avg. virality," and an invented "Priya S., Telugu Tech Podcaster."
Same intent, opposite credibility. **Swap fabricated stats for one real creator demo and you gain more
trust than any number.**

⚠️ Worth noting: Clipdify's "Trusted by" avatar cluster appears to be **stock Western faces** on a
product for regional Indic creators — the same credibility mistake you're making. It's a common tell;
don't copy it.

## What none of them do well (your opening)

- **Nobody shows the caption quality proof.** Every one of them *claims* accuracy; none show a
  side-by-side of correct vs broken conjuncts. That comparison is unclaimed territory.
- **Nobody's hero demonstrates long-form → clips for an Indic language.** Opus does it in English;
  Replix does Telugu but only captions. The gap — *a Telugu podcast becoming 10 Shorts, on the
  landing page* — is yours to take.
